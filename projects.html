Current Research Projects

PREDELLUM: Predictive Processing in Speech

As part of my PhD in Cognitive Neuroscience at the University of Salzburg, I contribute to the FWF-funded PREDELLUM project, which investigates how the human brain predicts the timing of upcoming speech and updates these predictions when expectations are violated. The project focuses on the interaction between the cerebellum and cortical language networks, combining magnetoencephalography (MEG), computational modelling, and non-invasive brain stimulation.

My Research

My work focuses primarily on Work Packages 1 and 3.

Work Package 1 – Predictive Processing in Speech

I investigate how temporal and semantic predictions interact during speech perception. Using MEG, we examine how the brain generates predictions, detects prediction errors, and updates internal models when speech unfolds over time.

Work Package 3 – Causal Manipulation of Predictive Brain Networks

A major focus of my research is combining transcranial alternating current stimulation (tACS) with simultaneous MEG to investigate the causal role of neural oscillations and cortico-cerebellar connectivity during speech prediction. By manipulating rhythmic brain activity, we aim to better understand how the cerebellum contributes to temporal prediction and explore potential applications for neurological disorders.

Methods

  • Magnetoencephalography (MEG)
  • Transcranial Alternating Current Stimulation (tACS)

  • Neural Oscillation Analysis
