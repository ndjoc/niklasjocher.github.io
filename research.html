Research

Predictive Processing

My primary research investigates how the brain generates predictions during speech perception and how predictive signals influence auditory processing.

The Cerebellum

I study the cerebellum beyond its traditional motor role, focusing on its contribution to cognition, prediction, and timing.

Magnetoencephalography (MEG)

MEG allows the investigation of neural activity with millisecond precision. I use MEG to study neural oscillations, connectivity, and large-scale brain dynamics.

Brain Stimulation (tACS)

Using transcranial alternating current stimulation, I investigate the causal role of oscillatory brain activity in perception and cognition.

Clinical Cognitive Neuroscience

I also work on applying MEG to investigate functional brain changes associated with neurological disorders, particularly Multiple Sclerosis.
