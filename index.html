Welcome

I am a PhD candidate in Cognitive Neuroscience at the University of Salzburg and a member of the Auditory Neuroscience Lab led by Prof. Dr. Nathan Weisz. My research focuses on understanding how the human brain predicts incoming sensory information and how these predictive mechanisms support speech perception and cognition.

Using magnetoencephalography (MEG), transcranial alternating current stimulation (tACS), and behavioral methods, I investigate neural oscillations, predictive processing, the role of the cerebellum, and functional brain alterations in neurological diseases such as Multiple Sclerosis.

Research Areas

Speech Prediction

Neural mechanisms underlying predictive processing during speech perception.

Cerebellar Neuroscience

Understanding how the cerebellum contributes to cognition and predictive computation.

MEG & Brain Stimulation

Combining MEG and tACS to investigate neural oscillations and brain dynamics.
