About Me

I am a psychologist with Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Psychology, specializing in Cognitive Neuroscience. I am currently pursuing a PhD in Cognitive Neuroscience at the University of Salzburg.

My research is conducted in the Auditory Neuroscience Laboratory, led by Prof. Dr. Nathan Weisz, where I investigate the neural mechanisms underlying predictive processing during speech perception.

My work combines magnetoencephalography (MEG), transcranial alternating current stimulation (tACS), behavioral experiments, and advanced signal analysis to understand how the brain predicts the timing of upcoming speech and how these predictions are updated when expectations are violated.

My current research focuses on the interaction between the cerebellum and cortical language networks, neural oscillations, and predictive coding. I also apply MEG to investigate functional brain changes in neurological disorders, particularly Multiple Sclerosis.
